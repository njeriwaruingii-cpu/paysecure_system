📚 Phishing & Payment Security

Learn how to identify scams and protect yourself from online fraud.

🎣

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted organisations — like banks, M-Pesa, or PayPal — to trick you into revealing passwords, PINs, or personal details. It usually arrives via SMS, email, or WhatsApp.

💳

Payment Message Fraud

Fraudsters send fake payment notifications that look real. They may claim your account is blocked, that you received money and must confirm, or that you need to reverse a transaction urgently. The goal is always to steal your credentials or money.

📱

M-Pesa & Mobile Money Scams

Common in Kenya. Scammers impersonate Safaricom, fake M-Pesa messages, or pose as agents asking you to "reverse" a sent amount. Safaricom will never ask for your PIN via SMS, call, or WhatsApp.

⚠ Warning Signs to Watch For

If a message has any of these, treat it as suspicious.

  • Message creates urgency — "Act immediately or your account will be closed."
  • Asks for your PIN, OTP, password, or personal ID number.
  • Contains a link to an unfamiliar or misspelled domain (e.g. safar1com.net).
  • Promises unexpected winnings, rewards, or free money.
  • Requests you send or transfer money to "confirm" or "reverse" a transaction.
  • Sender's number is unknown, hidden, or doesn't match the official shortcode.
  • Poor spelling, grammar, or formatting in the message.
  • Uses shortened links (bit.ly, tinyurl) instead of real domain names.

Study the difference between genuine and fraudulent messages.

Genuine M-Pesa Message

BJ896X Confirmed. Ksh500.00 sent to JOHN DOE 0712XXXXXX on 23/3/26 at 2:15 PM. New M-PESA balance is Ksh1,200.00. Transaction cost, Ksh11.00.

✔ Has a transaction code  ✔ No link  ✔ No PIN request  ✔ Comes from MPESA shortcode

🚨

Fake / Phishing Message

SAFARICOM ALERT: Your M-PESA account will be suspended. Verify your account immediately by clicking: http://mpesaa-verify.com/login Enter your PIN to avoid loss of funds.

✘ Fake domain  ✘ Asks for PIN  ✘ Urgency pressure  ✘ Suspicious link

🛡 How to Stay Safe

🔐

Never Share Your PIN or OTP

No legitimate bank, M-Pesa, or payment service will ever ask for your PIN, OTP, or password through SMS, email, WhatsApp, or a phone call. Ever. If someone asks — it is a scam.

🔗

Verify Links Before Clicking

Hover over any link before clicking. Check the domain carefully. Legitimate Safaricom pages use safaricom.com. Any variation like safar1com, safaricom.net, or shortened links should be avoided.

📞

Call the Official Number

If you receive a suspicious message claiming to be from your bank or M-Pesa, hang up or ignore it. Call the official customer care line directly: Safaricom: 100, Equity: 0763 000 000, KCB: 0711 087 000.
